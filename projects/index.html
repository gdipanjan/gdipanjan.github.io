My research projects in a nutshell

Here is a brief introduction to the research projects which I have worked on:

Collective swimming of Escherichia coli under geometric confinement : What makes our experience of driving on the highway very different compared to a bumper car ride? While driving on the highway, we try to avoid crashing into other cars. However, what makes a bumper car ride fun are the collisions with other cars. Now, in our experiments with swimming Escherichia coli bacteria, we were able to toggle their ability to "crash" into each other. This was done by constructing microfluidic devices of different thicknesses, where a pair of bacteria can crash or not crash into each other if the thickness is small or large respectively. We found that in the case where bacteria avoided crashes, they arranged themselves into long-range lanes travelling in mutually anti-parallel directions, similar to a two-lane highway. However, when the bacteria crashed into each otehr, we observed dynamic short-range clusters, reminiscent of those formed by colliding bumper cars. This work demonstrates that geometric confinement can be used as an effective tool for controlling the interaction rules between active particles, in this case the swimming bacteria, which can dramatically affect their emergent behavior at larger length scales.

Bayesian inference of optical trap stiffness and particle diffusion : Supervised by Ronojoy Adhikari at The Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai. The objective of this work was to formulate an exact Bayesian method for jointly estimating the mean regression rate and the diffusion coefficient of a Brownian particle in an optical trap. Knowledge of the particle diffusion coefficient is of immense utility, as it can be used to determine the viscosity of fluid samples in the nanoliter range, for which conventional viscometric methods cannot be used. Experimental data was provided by Ayan Banerjee of IISER, Kolkata. The overdamped Brownian motion was modelled by the Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process and simple analytical expressions for the maximum a posteriori estimates were derived using exact likelihoods. The advantages of the Bayesian method over traditional non-Bayesian fitting methods were discussed. The Bayesian methods used for this work are documented in this open-source software package.
