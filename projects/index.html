My research projects in a nutshell

Here is a brief introduction to the research projects which I have been a part of.

Bayesian inference of optical trap stiffness and particle diffusion : Supervised by Ronojoy Adhikari at The Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai.The objective of this work to formulate an exact Bayesian method for jointly estimating the mean regression rate and the diffusion coefficient of a Brownian particle in an optical trap. Knowledge of the particle diffusion coefficient is of immense utility, as it can be used to determine the viscosity of fluid samples in the nanoliter range, for which conventional viscometric methods cannot be used. Experimental data was provided by Ayan Banerjee of IISER, Kolkata. The overdamped Brownian motion was modelled by the Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process and simple analytical expressions for the maximum a posteriori estimates were derived using exact likelihoods. The advantages of the Bayesian method over traditional non-Bayesian fitting methods were discussed. The Bayesian methods used for this work are documented in this open-source software package.
